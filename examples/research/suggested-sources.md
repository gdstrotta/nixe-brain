# Suggested Sources — Research Knowledge Base

## Tier 1 — Ingest Immediately

**Your own notes and annotations**
Reading notes, paper summaries, margin annotations you've already written.
These are the highest-signal sources because they reflect your synthesis.

**Foundational papers in your field**
The 5–10 papers everyone in your field cites. The "canon". Ingest your
own summaries of each, focusing on methodology, key findings, and limitations.

**Your own prior work**
Published papers, theses, reports, conference presentations you've authored.
These generate the richest entity and concept pages because they reflect
your specific intellectual contribution.

---

## Tier 2 — Ingest Early

**Recent key papers (last 2–3 years)**
Papers that have shifted the conversation in your sub-field since the foundational
work. Focus on those that contradict, extend, or challenge Tier 1 papers.

**Methodology references**
Papers or books that define the methods you use. Key chapters, not full books.

**Review articles and meta-analyses**
High-quality systematic reviews that synthesize large bodies of literature.
These ingest well because they're already structured as synthesis.

---

## Tier 3 — Ingest Progressively

**Conference presentations and talks**
Transcripts or notes from talks in your field. Especially useful for
capturing emerging ideas before they're formalized as papers.

**Working papers and preprints**
Flag these clearly as unreviewed. They're valuable for cutting-edge ideas
but must be marked as provisional in the wiki.

---

## Suggested First Wave (5 sources)

1. The single most cited foundational paper in your research area
2. The most recent influential paper in your sub-field
3. Your own most recent paper or research summary
4. Your notes from the last conference or workshop you attended
5. The methodology paper most relevant to your current project
